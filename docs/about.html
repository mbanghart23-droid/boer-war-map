About This Project

Second Anglo-Boer War movement mapping, 1899–1902.

Project

This interactive map plots the movements of over 816 British, Boer, Colonial and Dominion forces across the Second Anglo-Boer War (October 1899 – May 1902). It focuses particularly on operations in the Eastern Cape, Free State, and Natal theatres. Each track represents a reconstructed movement sequence derived from contemporary accounts, official histories, and archival sources. The time-slider allows day-by-day animation of troop movements; the unit filter panel lets you isolate individual forces.

The project is part of the Valor Search archive of digitised military history records.

Data Sources

  • Arthur Conan Doyle, The Great Boer War (1900–1902) — day-by-day narrative used to reconstruct British movements
  • Thomas Pakenham, The Boer War (1979) — principal secondary source for all theatres
  • angloboerwar.com — unit orders of battle, commanding officers, battle accounts
  • South African Military History Society (SAMHS) journals — unit histories and operational detail
  • The National Archives (TNA) WO 126 nominal rolls — British casualty and enlistment records
  • National Archives of South Africa (NASA) — Boer commando records

Methodology

Movement records follow a movements.csv schema with the following fields:

FieldDescription
dateISO date of the event or movement
forceUnit name (regiment, commando, column)
from_locationDeparture place-name
to_locationDestination place-name
event_typemovement / engagement / garrison / capture / raid / …
event_nameNamed battle or operation, if applicable
killed / wounded / captured / missingCasualty figures where recorded
confidenceHigh · Medium · Low
notesSource citation or qualification

Locations are geocoded to known place-names and matched against a reference gazetteer. Where exact routes are unknown, gap-filling straight lines are used between confirmed positions.

Confidence levels:

  • High — contemporary primary source (official dispatch, war diary, regimental history)
  • Medium — secondary source corroboration (Doyle, Pakenham, SAMHS)
  • Low — inference or single-source record

Town guards and garrison units are recorded as static; they did not range out from their assigned towns. Western Cape events (Maritz Rebellion precursors, Smuts's Cape raid, Okiep) lie beyond this dataset's primary focus area.
