# Regiments & units in the Eastern Cape — Second Boer War (1899–1902)

*Compiled from public sources across two research passes (adversarial verification, ≥2/3 to confirm).
**Coverage:** comprehensive-but-not-exhaustive — the British fought here through ever-shifting flying
columns, and several angloboerwar.com unit pages block automated reading. Attestation graded:
✅ firmly tied to the eastern Cape · ◑ Cape/region service, eastern-Cape role likely · ❓ surfaced,
eastern-Cape service unconfirmed. Confidence/votes noted where from the verified set.*

## Two phases, two force structures
- **Phase 1 (late 1899 – early 1900) — frontier defence under Lt-Gen Gatacre.** A chain of frontier
  posts (Stormberg, Naauwpoort/Noupoort, Orange River, De Aar, Kimberley), **each holding a
  half-battalion of regular infantry + a section of Royal Engineers**; Stormberg, Naauwpoort and Orange
  River also each held **a Mounted Infantry company**; the **Cape Police** manned forward observation
  posts. *(Maurice, Official History — high. [P1])* Then the north-east Cape was cleared in early 1900 by
  **Brabant's Colonial Division**.
- **Phase 2 (1901–02) — anti-guerrilla flying columns under Lt-Gen French.** Self-contained mobile
  columns + a blockhouse/railway cordon.

## British flying columns (Phase 2) — confirmed commanders
**Gorringe · Crabbe · Henniker · Scobell · Doran · Kavanagh · Alexander · Crewe · Wyndham (Windham).**
Henniker's column was later absorbed into Doran's ("Doran's Column, late Lieut.-Colonel Henniker's").
*(high, 3-0 — Conan Doyle ch.35 + Kitchener's despatches via Creswicke Vol. VII. [C][CR])*
### Column compositions (resolved — 3rd-pass, verified)
- **Scobell's column** ✅ — 9th (Queen's Royal) Lancers (Lord Douglas Compton's sqn) + Cape Mounted
  Rifles (Capt J.F. Purcell's coy) + Imperial Yeomanry + ~3 guns. Destroyed Lötter, Groenkloof 5 Sep
  1901. *(high)* [9L][HS][HK]
- **Gorringe's column** ✅ (firmly eastern Cape) — **17th Lancers** (regiment under **Lt-Col Douglas
  Haig**, with 2 sqns at Tarkastad) + Cape Police + Cape Defence Force + Tasmanian Imperial Bushmen +
  5th Bty RFA (2 guns, 1 pom-pom). Stormberg→Tarkastad→Modderfontein/Cradock vs Smuts, Sep 1901; camped
  Hondeklip 16 Sep. *(high — SAMHS vol131rs; Welsh press)* [G131]
- **Crabbe's column** ✅ (comp. known; dated action central/SW Cape) — ~200 **Prince Alfred's
  (Volunteer) Guard Mounted Infantry** + **Marshall's Horse** (Maj Corbett) + 3 Imperial Yeomanry coys
  (38th/10th Bn, 104th/4th Bn, 57th/15th Bn). Clearing Cape Colony from May 1901; Driefontein Farm nr
  **Laingsburg** 9 Sep 1901 (central/SW Cape, not the Midlands). *(high)* [PAG][IY2]
- **Henniker→Doran column** ✅ (out of De Aar; chiefly western/central Cape) — 2 sqns **Warren's Mounted
  Infantry** + Imperial Yeomanry dets + **Victorian Imperial Regiment** + MI from Hanover Road + 2 guns
  **RHA M Battery**. *(high — Kitchener's despatches via angloboerwar)* [WMI][RHAM]
- **Crewe's, Kavanagh's, Alexander's, Wyndham's columns** ❓ — **no published order of battle** found in
  three passes; genuinely undocumented (do NOT invent). Wyndham's column was 17th-Lancers-associated
  (the Ruigtevlei/van Reenen action) but its full makeup isn't published.

### Newly confirmed eastern-Cape units (3rd pass)
- **Nesbitt's Horse** ✅ (was ❓) — raised Dec 1899 in the eastern Cape (~300, ~5 sqns); 1901 split, part
  in the **central/eastern Cape** (Colesberg–Maraisburg corridor, Ganna Hoek 21 Sep 1901); helped
  capture **Cmdt Malan near Sheldon ~27 May 1902**. *(high)* [NH]
- **Prince Alfred's Guard (Mounted Infantry)** ✅ (was ◑) — ~200 in Crabbe's column (one det. remained
  in the Kroonstad ORC district — corps was dispersed). *(high)* [PAG]
- **Marshall's Horse** ✅ — Crabbe's column; descends from the 1899 First City (Grahamstown) +
  Uitenhage Volunteer Rifles MI companies. *(high)* [GV]
- **Warren's Mounted Infantry** ✅ — Henniker/Doran column. **Victorian Imperial Regiment** (Australian)
  ✅ — Henniker/Doran column. **RHA M Battery** ✅ — 2 guns, Henniker/Doran column.
- **Imperial Yeomanry in the eastern Cape** ✅ — confirmed: **75th Coy / 18th Bn** under Parsons &
  Scobell vs Scheepers near **Aberdeen 5–6 Mar 1901**; 3 IY coys with Brabant's Horse (Cradock/Richmond,
  May–Jun 1901); 3 IY coys with Crabbe. *(high)* [IY2]
- **First City (Grahamstown) Volunteers** — no 1901–02 column attachment found; its operational thread
  runs through **Marshall's Horse**. *(high — explicit absence)* [GV]

## A. British regular cavalry
- **9th (Queen's Royal) Lancers** ✅ — Scobell's column; Cradock/Petersburg; Lötter. *(high)* A second
  squadron also operated with Brabant's Horse + Imperial Yeomanry in the Cradock/Richmond districts,
  May–Jun 1901, vs Malan. [9L][BH]
- **17th (Duke of Cambridge's Own) Lancers** ✅ — Gorringe's column; van Reenen pursuit; the
  Modderfontein/Elands River action near Tarkastad, 17 Sep 1901 (Smuts). *Note: a Conan-Doyle-sourced
  casualty/single-squadron phrasing of Modderfontein was **refuted 0-3**; use the Wikipedia figures (C
  Sqn; 29 killed, 41 wounded, 60 captured) from the movements dataset.*

## B. British regular infantry
- **2nd Northumberland Fusiliers** ✅ (~960) — Stormberg. *(high)* [St][P1]
- **2nd Royal Irish Rifles** ✅ (~840) — Stormberg. *(high)*
- **1st Royal Scots** ✅ — in Gatacre's district; remained in camp / guarded the railway at Molteno during the Stormberg attack. *(high)*
- **1st Royal Berkshire Regiment** ✅ (part) — Stormberg district; a mounted-infantry company took part (the main body never reached Molteno — an undelivered telegraph order). *(high)*
- **3rd Battalion Grenadier Guards** ✅ — Noupoort–De Aar blockhouse line; Kritzinger's capture, Dec 1901. *(high, prior pass)*
- **Dorsetshire Regiment / Royal Fusiliers (City of London)** ❓ — Cape service surfaced; eastern-Cape role not confirmed.

- **79th Battery, RFA** ◑ — Brabant's Colonial Division.
- **Royal Engineers — 33rd Company (part)** ✅ — Stormberg (one source says 12th Coy; Maurice/britishbattles give 33rd). *(high)*

## D. Imperial Yeomanry & irregular horse
- **Imperial Yeomanry** ✅ — three companies in the Cradock/Richmond districts (May–Jun 1901, vs Malan); also with Scobell's column at times. *(high)* [BH][HS]
- **Nesbitt's Horse** ◑ — Cape colonial irregular horse (a unit page exists; eastern-Cape role to pin down).
- **Kitchener's Fighting Scouts** ❓ — to confirm for this theatre.

## E. Cape colonial units — the distinctive local layer
- **Cape Mounted Riflemen (CMR)** ✅ — Scobell's column; Brabant's Colonial Division. *(high)*
- **Cape Police / Cape Mounted Police** ✅ — forward observation posts along the frontier from the
  outset. *(high)* *(A claim that they acted as guides at Stormberg specifically was refuted 1-2.)*
- **Brabant's Horse (1st & 2nd Regiments)** ✅ — raised 5 Nov 1899 (~1,200, two regts) in the
  **Queenstown–Dordrecht** district; De Montmorency's 160-man detachment reached Molteno on 10 Dec
  1899; core of the Colonial Division. *(high)* [BH]
- **Frontier Mounted Rifles (FMR)** ✅ — eastern-Cape corps (str. 229), embodied Oct 1899, stationed
  **Barkly East, Cathcart, Molteno, Indwe**; served under Gatacre then Brabant; Dordrecht, 5 Mar 1900
  (3 killed, 7 wounded). *(high)* [FMR]
- **Midland Mounted Rifles (MMR)** ✅ — locally-raised (Phase 2), the **Cradock–Graaff-Reinet** zone;
  60 captured by Kritzinger between Cradock and Graaff-Reinet (Maraisburg), 21 Jun 1901; checked Lötter
  ~2 Sep 1901. *(high)* [MMR]
- **Kaffrarian Rifles** ✅ — East London / King William's Town (ex-Buffalo Corps, 1876); Colonial
  Division. *(high for Colonial-Division membership)*
- **Prince Alfred's Guard** ◑ — Port Elizabeth (ex-Port Elizabeth Rifles, 1856); a Mounted Infantry page exists. *(attested as a corps; eastern-Cape field role to firm up)*
- **First City (Grahamstown Volunteers)** ◑ — Grahamstown (1875).
- **Queenstown Volunteers / Queenstown Rifle Volunteers** ◑ — Queenstown. *(a Stormberg-battle role was refuted 1-2.)*
- **District Mounted Troops / District Mounted Rifles** ◑ — locally-raised district defence.
- **Town Guards** ◑ — local town defence (e.g. the armed Jansenville Town Guard fort Smuts bypassed).
- **Colonial Division** ✅ — umbrella formation under **Brig-Gen Edward Brabant** (Jan 1900): the two
  Brabant's Horse regiments + CMR + Kaffrarian Rifles + FMR; cleared the NE Cape, Dordrecht (Feb 1900),
  Aliwal North (11 Mar 1900). *(high)*

## F. Overseas colonial contingents
- **Tasmanian 4th (2nd Imperial) Contingent** ✅ — joined Scobell's column 19 May 1901, then under Gorringe. *(prior pass)*
- New Zealand & NSW Lancers — confirmed on the **central** Cape (Colesberg) front, see §G; their presence in the strictly-eastern columns is unconfirmed. ❓

## G. Central / northern-border Cape front (ADJACENT — not strictly eastern Cape)
French's Colesberg-front force (Nov 1899–Feb 1900), based **Naauwpoort** with posts at Arundel/Rensburg
— geographically the central/northern border, listed separately so it isn't conflated with the eastern
Cape. Infantry: **2nd Berkshire, 1st Royal Irish, 2nd Wiltshire, 2nd Worcester, 1st Essex, 1st
Yorkshire, 1st Suffolk**; cavalry: **10th Hussars, 6th Dragoon Guards, Inniskillings (6th Dragoons),
Composite Household Regiment, New Zealanders, NSW Lancers, Rimington's Guides**; artillery: **R & O
Batteries RHA, 4th RFA, section 37th Howitzer Battery**. *(high, 3-0 — Conan Doyle ch.14 + Maurice. [C14][P1])*
